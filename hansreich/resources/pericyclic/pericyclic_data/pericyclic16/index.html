  The Cope Rearrangement for most systems requires temperatures in the neighborhood of 250 °C, and the equilibrium constant tends to be fairly evenly balanced, so synthetic applications are infrequent. The rates for systems bearing a hydroxy group (oxy-Cope) can be greatly accelerated by conversion to an alkoxide (anionic oxy-Cope), and such reactions have found a few applications in synthesis.
