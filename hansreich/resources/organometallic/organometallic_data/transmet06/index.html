Olefin Metathesis

This reaction is one of the most-used methods to make double bonds in current syntheses. The 2005 Nobel prize was given to Yves Chauvin (who established the mechanism), Richard R. Schrock (who developed the first really useful catalysts, which are Mo-centered), and Robert H. Grubbs (who developed the Ruthenium-centered catalysts that are usually employed in the laboratory now, and usually called Grubbs I and II).
