C-Si groups as C-OH group precursors.

Appropriately activated C-Si bonds can be cleaved in a Baeyer-Villiger-type oxidation (Tamao-Fleming Oxidation) using peroxide, peracid, or amine oxide as oxidants. Trialkyl silyl groups are cleaved with great difficulty, it is usually necessary to have one electronegative atom (F, O, N) on silicon. Me2PhSi is frequently used because the Ph group can be removed by protonolysis or brominolysis to give an SiR2-OH or SiR2-Br group, which is subject to oxidation.
