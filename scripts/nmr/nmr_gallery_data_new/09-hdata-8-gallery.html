NMR Spectra Gallery

Explanation

These spectra were collected by Hans J. Reich over several decades of research and teaching at the University of Wisconsin, Madison. Many were used as examples, exercises and exam questions (marked Q0, Q1, Q2 or Q3) in an NMR course (Chem 605) and in undergraduate organic chemistry courses which featured an introduction to NMR spectrosopy. The spectra are collected here to provide a resource for researchers and instructors interested in learning, teaching and applying NMR spectroscopy.

The Source of the NMR spectra, when available, is given on the spectra. Many thanks to the individuals who donated these.

 

Many of the spectra are marked "Digitized hard copy." This involved creating a vector based spectrum by electronically "tracing" a bitmap of the hard copy. Every attempt was made to create accurate reproductions of the original spectra. However, because of imperfections in the digitizing process (done with WINPLT) and occasional problems with the clarity of the hard copy, such spectra may occasionally lack some very small peaks or some details of line shape may not be perfect. Line separations (J values, chemical shifts) may also be off by a few tenths of a Hz. For 13C NMR spectra, shifts may be off by 0.1 ppm. For a few of the spectra cosmetic adjustments (baseline drift correction, artifact removal) were made. A few of the spectra (those at 60 and 100 MHz) date back to continuous wave spectrometers. For these the "ringing" pattern was usually not reproduced.
