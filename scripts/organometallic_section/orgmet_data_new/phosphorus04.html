The Mitsunobu Reaction - Oxidation-Reduction Condensation

  The Mitsunobu and related reactions use a redox process to perform dehydration reactions - the oxygen of the water is typically removed by oxidation of a phosphine to a phosphine oxide, the two hydrogens reduce some component of the reaction. In the Mitsunobu reaction this is an ester or amide of azodicarboxylic acid, but many other oxidants are used (e.g., CCl4, CBr4, RSSR, RS-CN).
  For successful reactions the pKA of the nucleophile should be <11.
