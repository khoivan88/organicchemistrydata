  An electrocyclic reaction is defined as the formation of a single bond between the ends of a linear systems of π electrons, or its reverse. Such a reaction involves the conversion of a π bond to a σ bond, or σ to a π bond.

Some types of electrocyclic reactions:


 

  Orbital symmetry considerations place severe constraints of the stereochemistry of electrocyclic reactions. Thus the thermal cyclobutene to butadiene ring opening, a 4-electron process, occurs in a conrotatory fashion (Moebius transition state) whereas the 6e cyclohexadiene-hexatriene interconversion occurs in a disrotatory fashion (Huckel transition state). The electrcyclic reaction of an octatetraene is again conrotatory. In many cases photochemical versions of these reactions can be performed, for these the stereochemical outcome is usually reversed.
