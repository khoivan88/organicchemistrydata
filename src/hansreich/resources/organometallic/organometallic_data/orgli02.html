Preparation. Sulfones are easily prepared by a variety of synthetic procedures:

· Oxidation of sulfides and sulfoxides

· Nucleophilic substitution of halides and tosylates by sodium arenesulfinate

· Conjugate addition of sodium arenesulfinate to a,b-unsaturated carbonyl compounds

· Alkylation of lithiosulfones

· Conjugate addition to vinyl and alkynyl sulfones

· Cycloaddition of SO2 to dienes

  

Metalation. All types of sulfones (1ï¿½, 2ï¿½, 3ï¿½, allyl, vinyl) which have a-hydrogens metalate easily with n-BuLi or LiNiPr2, and the anions show good nucleophilicity. Commonly used electrophiles are alkyl halides and tosylates, epoxides, aldehydes, ketones and esters.

Subsequent Transformations. The products of reaction of metalated sulfones with electrophiles can be used in various ways:

· Reductive elimination of b-oxy and b-halo sulfones (Julia olefination)

· Oxidation of b-oxy sulfones to b-keto sulfones and desulfonylation to ketones

· Reductive desulfonylation with Al/Hg

· Metalation/oxidation to form ketones

· If cleavage of the C-S bond gives a stabilized cation, some sulfones can behave as C-electrophiles

· b-Elimination to give olefins if b-hydrogens are acidic
