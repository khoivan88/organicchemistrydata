Overview on Reactions of Organolithium Reagents

  1. Addition to aldehydes and ketones. This reaction is limited only by occasional interference from other reactive functionalities (e.g. β-dicarbonyl compounds, pKa ca 10 are often deprotonated in preference to carbonyl addition). Dactylolide: Louis, I.; Hungerford, N. L.; Humphries, E. J.; McLeod, M. D. Org. Lett. 2006, 8, 1117. DOI

 

Cycloclavine: Petronijevic, F. R.; Wipf, P. J. Am. Chem. Soc. 2011, 133, 7704–77079. DOI

 

  2. Addition to amides, especially N,N-dimethylformamide and Weinreb amides (N-methoxy-N-methyl amides). ACRL Toxin IIIb: Munchhof, M. J.; Heathcock, C. H. J. Org. Chem. 1994, 59, 7566. DOI

 

  3. Reaction with alkylating agents, including epoxides. These SN2 reactions are usually limited to primary centers. Cuprate reagents are a better choice for addition of alkyl and vinyl groups (pKa > 40), lithium works better for more stabilized anions (allyl, benzyl, α-thio such as lithiodithiane, enolates, etc). 7-Hydroxy-Myoporone: Reich, H. J.; Shah, S. K.; Gold, P. M.; Olson, R. E. J. Am. Chem. Soc., 1981, 103, 3112. DOI

 

  4. Reaction with Imines. Aldimines generally work well, ketimines sometimes suffer from competitive deprotonation. Porantherine: Corey, E. J.; Balanso, R. D. J. Am. Chem. Soc. 1974, 96, 6516–6517. DOI

 

  5. Reaction with aziridines. Aziridines, pareticularly those with an electron-withdrawing substituent on N (like sulfonyl, sulfinyl, carbonyl, or phosphoryl) are reactive as SN2 substrates if one of the carbons is primary. Poison Frog Alkaloid (-)-205B: Smith, A. B. III; Kim, D. S. Org. Lett. 2005, 7, 3247. DOI


 

  6. Formation of other organometallic reagents, Virtually all M-X bonds can be replaced by M-R bonds by reaction with R-Li (nucleophilic substitution at the M center). Particularly organocopper reagents are usually prepared this way, but also Ce, Mg, Zn, B, Al, Si, Sn, Ti, Fe and many other organometallic compounds can be formed. Although R-Li species are among the most nucleophilic (and readily available) of all organometallic compounds, transmetalation often provides reagents with improved stereo-, regio- or chemo-selectivity.

Vincorine: Horning, B. D.; MacMillan, D. W. C. J. Am. Chem. Soc. 2013, 135, 6442-6445. DOI


 

  7. Deprotonations (metalations) - mostly n-BuLi, s-BuLi and t-BuLi are used here. Any compound with a pKa below about 40 can in principle be deprotonated to form a new lithium reagent (see above for an example). In particular, lithium acetylides are almost always prepared this way, and many arenes can be ortho-metalated to orm aryllithiums.

  8. Metal-halogen exchange - n-BuLi, s-BuLi and t-BuLi react with vinyl, aryl and some alkyl iodides and bromides to form new lithium reagents. An example where the formed vinylllithium react intramoleculary was used in a synthesis of Brunsvigine: Sha, C.-K.; Hong, A.-Wei.; Huang, C. M. Org. Lett. 2001, 3, 2177. DOI
