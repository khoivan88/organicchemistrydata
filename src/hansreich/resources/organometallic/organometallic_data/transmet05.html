Nobel-Prize-winning chemistry
R. Heck, 2010

The Heck reaction couples alkenyl or aryl iodides, bromides, or triflates to the vinyl carbon atoms of alkenes. Carbonate, and other ligands, such as halides or phosphines are often added. It proceeds through a syn oxidative addition of R PdX followed by a syn reductive elimination of (X)Pd H (which can be at a different carbon), forming an alkene with a new CC bond:


 

The Heck Reaction has high functional group tolerance and is especially useful for making congested polycyclic frameworks and quaternary centers. There can be regioselectivity problems.
