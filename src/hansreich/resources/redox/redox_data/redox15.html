Active Manganese Dioxide Oxidation in Organic Chemistry
  Fatiadi, A. J. Synthesis 1976, 65-104. Fatiadi, A. J. Synthesis 1976, 133-167.

  There are several high-oxidation state manganese oxidants used in organic synthesis (MnO2, KMnO4, BaMnO4) the most commonly used is activated manganese dioxide, which specifically oxidizes allylic and benzyllic alcohols to enones or enals under very mild conditions. It is a heterogeneous oxidation, with the reagent used in superstoichiometric amounts, so it is poorly suited for large-scale oxidations.
