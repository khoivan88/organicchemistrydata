  If the 6-membered cyclic Diels-Alder transition state can be considered as benzene-like, then 1,3-dipolar cycloadditions are cyclopentadienide like. Here a lone pair replaces one of the double bonds of the diene 4-electron component. Just as the neutral 5-memered ring aromatic compounds have at least one heteroatom, most useful 1,3-dipolar cycloadditions have at least one heteroatom in the 1,3-dipole component.


 

Common 1,3-Dipoles

  A large family of 1,3-dipoles can be constructed conceptually by the addition of the lone pair of a π-bonded 2-atom fragment to the empty orbital of a carbene, nitrene or oxene.


 

  Many of the double-bonded 1,3-dipoles have stable cyclic analogs, which can sometimes serve as precursors for the dipoles by thermal or photochemical ring-opening
