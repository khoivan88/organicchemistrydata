  The Diels-Alder reaction is a reversible process. The higher entropy of starting materials compared to products means that, in principle, all Diels-Alder reaction can be reverted to starting diene and dienophile at sufficiently high temperatures. Reversibility of cycloadditions can be one of the causes of loss of endo-selectivity. Aromatic dienes (furan, anthracene) undergo especially facile cycloreversion reactions.

  One use of retro-Diels-Alder reactions is for the preparation of reactive olefins or other π-bonds by means of flash vacuum pyrolysis. Some dienes which appear to be especially suitable this purpose are anthracene, 9,10-dimethylanthracene, cyclopentadiene, and dimethylfulvene. Some dienophiles involved in fragmentations are CO2, N2 and R-C≡N.
